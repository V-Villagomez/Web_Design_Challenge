Welcome

Data is more powerful when we share it with others! The purpose for this project is to share statistics relating to the famous pilgrimage in Spain, The Way to Santiago de Compostela or The Way of Saint James. The Camino has taken many names, one reason being that there are several routes that lead to Santiago de Compostela, Spain. People from all over the world walk the Camino (Way) de Santiago each year.

According to Wikipedia, the Way of Saint James was one of the most important Christian pilgrimages dating back to the Middle Ages. Legend has it that the remains of apostle Saint James the Great are buried in the Cathedral of Santiago de Compostela, Galicia.

Since the late Middle Ages, the Pilgrimage to Santiago de Compostela continues. How wonderful is it that this tradition continues. While the Camino’s tradition began for religious purposes, today, people walk it for several other reasons such as, spiritual, adventure, health, miracles, and the list goes on. The Camino is over 500 miles in distance.

Join me in exploring Pilgrimage data from 2004 to 2018. Data was manually collected as provided by the Oficina del Peregrino’s Statistics website. We will take a look at the total number of pilgrims over the years, gender participation, number of pilgrims per country, and the average number of pilgrims per Camino Route.

I hope you enjoy learning about the Camino. Walking the Camino had an immense impact in my life, and I wish to share it with you.
