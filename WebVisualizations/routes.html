Camino Routes Stats


The Pilgrims Stats shows that the population of pilgrims who walk the Camino de Santiago, grows each year. By looking at the graph, beginning in 2005, there was a steady incline, but in 2010, there was a major increase. An increase of approximately 86.5% from the year prior, 145,877 vs. 272,135 pilgrims. The reason for the surge was due to the Holy Year. According to the Oficina del Peregrino, "In the years in which the Feast of St. James (25th July) falls on a Sunday, that year is celebrated as a Holy Year. Therefore there is a Holy Year every 6, 5, 6 and 11 years. The last Holy Year was in 2010 and the next is in 2021. During Holy Years there is a considerable increase in the numbers of pilgrims to Santiago, who may enter the Cathedral by the “Holy Door” which is only opened during that year."
